In the following we would like to inform you on news relating to the well known multiprobe for water quality monitoring produced by the company Hydrolab.

Hydrolab

Nowadays Hydrolab produces multicomponent probes of the former "3" series and of the new "4a" series replacing since April 1999 "4" series. Below you may find main features of individual instruments and their capabilities:

3 series consists of H 20 Multiprobe, DataSonde 3 and Scout 2 Display.

H20

H 20 is designed for transfer of determined parameters via serial interface RS 232 or SDI 12 (in USA very extended addressable network connection of instruments). The instrument is always equipped with sensors of temperature and conductivity with its calculation to salinity and solid particulates content.

Optional sensors are the following:

  • Dissolved oxygen in the range of 0 - 20 mg/l or 0 - 200 % saturation

  • pH 0 - 14

  • Redox 2.000 mV - 12.000 mV

  • Depth 0 - 100 m or 0 - 200 m

  • Vented level 0 - 10 m with pressure compensation

DataSonde 3

DataSonde 3 is the same as H 20, however, it is equipped with memory. Its typical regime is recording of detected parameters in preset time intervals into memory with consequent data transcription (transfer) into PC. The instrument allows to carry out regime measurements in duration of several months.

Scout 2 Display Unit

Scout 2 Display Unit is designed for display of data monitored by H 20 and DataSonde 3 on LCD display in expedition measurements.

4 series consists of MiniSonde 4, DataSonde 4 and Surveyor 4

MiniSonde 4

MiniSonde 4 has 1.75" diameter. Lower end of the multiprobe comprises temperature sensor that is always present, port for depth or vented level monitor and 4 ports for sensors mounting. Vented level monitor has the range of 0 - 10 m. Depth meter has ranges of 0 - 25, 100 or 200 m.

As for other sensors specification, we have to bear in mind limitations following from 4 mounting ports for their installation:

  • Conductivity sensor or combined dissolved oxygen and conductivity sensor.
  • Dissolved oxygen sensor requires a stirrer in the case it is not placed in water stream with minimum flow velocity 25 m/s.
  • Oxygen sensor not requiring stirrer, which is still supplied with 3 series, is not offered with 4a series (due to slower response, the reason, why prevailing number of customers chose sensor with stirrer).

  • pH and redox (ORP) sensor. There are several possibilites: We can use combined sensor including measuring and reference pH electrode and ORP electrode. Electrode filling interval is approximately 3 weeks. If we sacrifice another measuring port, we can have (for the same price) a separate reference electrode, filling life-time of which is 2 months.
  • Turbidity meter.
  • Total dissolved gas in water sensor, usually used for monitoring of air content above saturation level, when gas bubbles harmful for water animals are created. Sensor can be used for depths up to 40 m. With deeper immersion damage of sensitive membrane can occur.
  • Ion-specific sensors, where we should be aware of relatively short life-time (approximately 6 months), with difference to other sensors, life-time of which exceeds 2 years (filling of pH electrode cannot be considered as life-time limitation). At the moment producer offers sensors of ammonia, nitrates and chlorine.
DataSonde 4

DataSonde 4. Due to its larger dimensions it allows installation of more sensors. Apart from temperature and depth/vented level sensors there are 8 sensor ports. DataSonde 4 has broader possibilities of some sensors application.

In the first place we will deal with pH and ORP sensors. Above described possibilities are extended for even more capacious reference electrode, mounted into a sensor port, but exceeding outer instrument dimensions (outline). It has the same price as other pH electrodes; what should one consider is enlarged cross section profile of the instrument. The last pH electrode we want to mention is single teflon electrode, life-time of which is usually 2 - 4 years (warranty 2 years). Electrode works reliably also in water with conductivity lower than 50 m S/cm, when classic electrodes do not produce good results.

Turbidity sensor

Turbidity sensor. Its construction is made according to ISO 7027 and designed for expedition turbidity in profile measurement. If we wish to operate instrument permanently, the most appropriate way is to use the instrument with orifice and teflon wiper for optics silica windows. For particulates content in clear water determination, when turbidimeter shows only natural nephelometric water response, transmisometer should be used. This determines light absorbtion at the optical length 10 or 25 cm and is therefore extraordinarily sensitive to even minimum pollution. It is mounted on the side of the instrument with side cable transition through enclosure.

PAR sensor

PAR sensor is designed for determination of Photosyntetically Active Radiation. It is a glass flask mounted on the probe side with cable transition through enclosure.

Application: Firstly radiation caused by sunlight directly above water surface is sensed. Step-by-step immersion serves to find out level of radiation penetration into depth. It is not the task of this contribution to describe devastating influence of water pollution prohibiting radiation from penetration into depth with all consequencies for water ecosystems. For continuous installation the instrument can be equipped with two sensors, where one of them is fixed on floating buoy and provides reference signal according to current sunlight.

The last sensor we would like to describe in this contribution is electrode for chlorophile determination. It is a miniature fluorescence sensor equipped with sample pump to provide sample flow through sensing pipe. Sensor and pump are mounted outside on the instrument enclosure.

At this place we would like to remind you of the fact that both described 4 series probes are supplied with model indication 4a since April 1999. Difference lies in different connector for cable connection and limitation of interface units positions on the instrument electronics mother board. In 4a models interfaces for connection of standard sensors are on the mother board placed permanently.

MiniSonde 4a multiprobe is in basic version powered by four "AA" batteries. If we are going to operate instrument also under cool weather, we recommend to use extended enclosure for 8 batteries.

DataSonde 4a is powered by 8 "C" batteries, Both probes can be externally powered via adapter. Probes communicate via RS 232 or SDI 12 interfaces, both of which are installed (with difference to 3 series, where interface should be specified before delivery). For regime measurements probes can be supplemented with memory chips.

Surveyor 4

In conclusion, some words relating to Surveyor 4 instrument. It is a miniature logger with graphic 3" LCD display presenting all detected parameters. In basic version 300 readings can be stored into memory (with transcription possibility into Excel or Lotus environment in PC). As an option user can choose memory extension for 375.000 readings in 24 user defined files, GPS (global positioning system) for recording of measuring site coordinates and atmospheric pressure sensor.
